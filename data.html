Data and Challenges

Want to participate in the hackathon but dont have an idea of something to build? No prob, Bob. We've come up with some ideas and challenges for you using data from the Tri-Co. Feel free to pursue any idea you'd like though! You are not limited to the challenges or data listed here.

Potential Challenges
  • An online game incorporating materials from the collections of the Tri-Co.
  • A mashup generator of archival text, image, video, and sound from our collections.
  • A dashboard of library usage statistics that we can run monthly to see how our collections are being used.
  • A way to make locating relevant materials and sources for a research project easier.
Potential Data

The following are data that the TRI-Co has made available (Google Folder):

  • A (giant) CSV file (or text file) containing a record for each book within the circulating collections at the three colleges. Each record will indicate how many times it has circulated since 1990, how many times it circulated this year, and all of the bibliographic data as well as the location of the book itself, subject headings, etc. Well also include links to APIs from national-level cultural organizations and/or full text providers (Project Gutenberg, Internet Archive, etc.). (Field Explanation
  • A CSV file (or text file) containing records for every DVD in our collection and links to the associated national level APIs about film.
  • An API for Dspace and a list of the collections that are accessible there. These include:
    • 74 videos from the Swarthmore College Archives
    • Haverford College senior theses (e.g., 789 Political Science theses dating back to 1965)
    • 222 Honor Council abstracts from Haverford, dating back to 1984
  • An API for Content DM and a list of collections that are accessible there. Interesting collections Include:
    • Bryn Mawr Colleges historic maps collection
    • There are many, many, many collections of archival college photos, records, newspapers, letters, etc., listed here.
  • A list of the various social media presences of our institutions and the APIs for those sites.
  • A collection of common APIs in the library and cultural heritage world.
